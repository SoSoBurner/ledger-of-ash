# Player-Safe World Guide

This guide is a derived player-safe surface subordinate to canon. It avoids hidden contradiction packages and deep secret-state material while preserving the world’s public logic.

## What ordinary people know
People understand the world as one shaped by gods, dangerous history, strange celestial mechanics, and very real regional differences. Travel, law, religion, weather, and trade do not feel the same from one polity to the next.

## What changes by region
In Shelk, presentation, craft, and public harmony matter. In Roaz, legality and enforcement are felt quickly. In Panim, rite and afterlife consequence shape daily trust. In Soreheim, contribution and industrial discipline carry social weight. In Sheresh, magical capability is ordinary but contamination remains a constant concern. In Psanan, heat, descent, and house authority shape survival itself.

## How adventurers are read
Adventurers are rarely invisible. They are read through clothing, speech, foreignness, equipment, divine alignment, magical handling, and whether they treat local procedures as real. In some places that means curiosity. In others it means scrutiny.

## What matters most in play
Respect the local laws and visible power. Pay attention to who belongs, who is out of place, and what pressure the place is hiding badly. The world rewards people who read institutions, routes, rites, and logistics instead of acting as though every region is generic fantasy space.
