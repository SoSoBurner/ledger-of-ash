# Fun Review — Ledger of Ash
**Date:** 2026-04-23  
**Scope:** Entire game, Stage 1 & 2 release  
**Skill:** game-design:fun-review

---

## Fun Type Classification

**Primary:** Mimicry + Alea — inhabiting an archetype inside a guild-procedural world; DC checks preserve meaningful uncertainty without pure luck.  
**Secondary:** Agon — competing against scaling DCs, building toward thresholds, optimizing build.  
**Minimal by design:** Ilinx — text RPG, no kinetic layer. Correct scope decision.  
**Player motivation profile:** Explorers (discover secrets, map suppression) + Achievers (level, unlock companions, complete stages).

**Temporal fun type:** Targets Type 1 (flow in moment) with Type 2 elements (grueling investigation → satisfying revelation). Risk: Type 3 (players bounce before payoff) if Stage 1 onboarding is thin or Stage 2 is sparse.

---

## Flow Prerequisites

**Skill-challenge balance:** DC scaling calibrated. Three-path approach routing rewards build identity. ✅  
**Sense of control:** Seed injection telegraphs boss encounters. ✅  
**Clear feedback — macro gap:** Individual outcomes visible; macro progress (what am I working toward?) weak. ⚠️

---

## Incentive Chains

**Micro (per encounter):** Fully visible. Action → roll → outcome → journal update → flag → unlock. ✅  
**Meso (per stage):** stageProgress threshold invisible in HUD. Player can't see how close they are to boss trigger. Anticipation engine requires Y to be visible. ⚠️  
**Macro (across stages):** Weakest link. Conspiracy arc deliberately withholds the answer to "what's going on?" but doesn't replace it with a visible player goal. Players need a surrogate target even when the mystery is suppressed. ❌

---

## Tension

**Stage 1:** Thin. Encounter-level DC risk is difficulty, not competing objectives. "But" test fails: no structural competing pull in Stage 1. The economy tension (training vs. items) is never foregrounded. Stage 1 runs on Mimicry/Explorer enjoyment, not mechanical tension. ⚠️  
**Stage 2:** Substantially stronger. Watchfulness/rival clock squeeze creates genuine competing objectives. Problem: depends on both loops being legible simultaneously. ⚠️  
**Boss encounters:** Strongest tension moments — satisfy all three diagnostics (goal known, stakes understood, next steps visible via approach paths). ✅

---

## Layered Aesthetics

**Visceral:** Typography + CSS only. No sound/animation. Thin by design — correct architectural constraint. The 60–90 word result cap is correct visceral pacing.  
**Mechanical:** DC system, item chains, companion system, watchfulness, rival clock. Game's primary strength. ✅  
**Fantasy:** Guild-procedural conspiracy thriller. Suppression-as-absence. Strong and distinctive. ✅

---

## Surprise and Scope Reveals

Correct structure exists — Ledger document reveal mid-Stage 4. Bounded rules → expanded scope, not rule violation. **Problem: Stage 4 payoff is absent from a Stage 1–2 release.** The Stage 1→2 bridge arcs and Stage 2 antechamber (stageProgress[2] ≥ 12) are the structural positions for a Stage 1–2 scope reveal. Whether the prose delivers an "oh, I see" moment determines whether Stage 2 feels like escalation or more of Stage 1.

## Player Clarity

| Question | Stage 1 | Stage 2 |
|----------|---------|---------|
| What am I trying to do? | ❌ Weak — no stated macro goal | ⚠️ Better — confront rivals, stay ahead of clock |
| What are the stakes? | ⚠️ Local only | ✅ Clearer — watchfulness, companions, boss gate |
| What comes next? | ⚠️ Micro only | ✅ Better — boss threshold exists |

**Critical gap:** Stage 1 has no visible surrogate macro goal. "I've seen too many missing records to ignore this pattern" is a character motivation — needs to translate to a player goal the game can reference.

## Two Highest-Leverage Changes

1. **Stage 2 content escalation pass** — most urgent fun risk. 27% of Stage 1 density means the incentive chain that sustained Stage 1 flow loses density exactly when it should build toward climax. Players report this as "the game gets thin" — which is retention language for a broken incentive chain. No system polish fixes content density.

2. **Give Stage 1 a visible surrogate macro goal** — not the conspiracy's name, but a stated player intention that answers "what am I working toward?" even while the mystery runs beneath. The goal and the mystery are different things. The game conflates them, and players pay the clarity cost.
