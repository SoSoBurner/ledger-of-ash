# Harvest Circle — Text RPG Flavor Packet

**Parent polity:** Soreheim Alliance (Eternal Lands)
**Type:** Market harvest town
**Truth layer:** reconciled_canon

## Atmosphere

The Circle is exactly what the name says: a wide ring of packed earth with the grain tables set in sectors around it, each sector marked by the colors of the patron-family that controls those stalls. Convoy lanes feed in from four directions, and the clerks' shelter sits at the hub, roofed, ledger tables out in the open where anyone can see what is being written. At harvest the ring is crowded to the edges. In off-season it is still crowded, because the redistribution cycle never stops.

The colors are the first thing a visitor reads. A family's banners hang above their stall block, and the banners tell you whose stall you are standing near before the stallholder opens his mouth. Honesty is public here — loads are declared aloud at the clerk's table, weights are called out, and a family whose declared weight does not match the clerk's weight does not do that twice.

## Scene Openers

1. **Dawn, quota clerks' table.** A convoy from an outlying field has pulled in overnight and is queued at the hub. The lead driver is calling out loads to the clerk one wagon at a time. A patron-family's factor is standing close enough to hear and is writing his own count in a smaller book; the two counts will be compared at the end of the line and one of them will need to explain any gap.
2. **Midmorning, festival broker's bench.** The broker has a line three deep — a cooper asking about a stall for a debt-settlement feast, a widow asking whether her late husband's pledge still stands, and a stranger who has not yet made clear which kind of business he brought. The broker is listening to all three at once and has not yet decided which he will take first.
3. **Afternoon, stall block transition.** A sector is clearing for the next family's rotation in. The outgoing family's porters are pulling their banner poles down; the incoming family's children are already laying their cloth. A senior cousin from each family supervises the handover and they are polite to each other because the whole Circle is watching.
4. **Evening, unity rite at the ring's edge.** A small procession is setting offerings at the marker-stones that ring the outer edge of the packed earth. It is not a temple event. The participants are stallholders and convoy families, and the rite is both devotion and public acknowledgement of debts paid, favors kept, and work pledged for the coming cycle.

## Social Register

- Declare loads honestly at the clerk's table. The system is honesty first, verification second, and a gap between the two is not a mistake — it is a position.
- Speak to families by their color, not their surname, unless you already know their surname. "Red-banner, your porter's waiting" is correct. Guessing a surname wrong is not forgiven quickly.
- The broker is addressed first. Going around a festival broker to talk directly to a family about a stall is a social miscalculation people will notice.
- Do not handle produce you are not buying. Do not stand in a sector you are not trading in without a reason. Do not tell a clerk he has miscounted — tell him you would like to hear the count again.
- Favors are public. A favor done quietly is still repaid publicly, usually at the next unity rite, because the whole point is that everyone in the Circle knows who is owed what.

## Signature Details

- **The banner colors.** Each patron-family has a color and a small geometric mark sewn into their banners. Children in the family are taught to recognize every other family's mark before they can write. A stranger who learns two or three family colors becomes three degrees more legible to the Circle.
- **The chalk board at the clerk's shelter.** A long slate running the length of the ledger tables, chalked each morning with the day's convoy intake and erased each evening after the counts reconcile. A number left on the board overnight is a public statement that a count did not reconcile, and it is the first thing everyone reads at dawn.
- **The unity-rite markers.** Rough stones, waist-high, set at intervals around the outer edge of the ring. Offerings are small — a pinch of grain, a wound cloth, a coin — and they accumulate through the day. Clearing the markers is the closing ceremony of each cycle, done by a rotation of families.

## Complication Seed

A convoy has arrived with a declared load that reconciles perfectly with the clerks' weighing — too perfectly, by the practiced eye of the senior clerk, who has been running the hub table for eleven years and knows the weight of a road-settled wagon to within a handful. She suspects the convoy was re-weighted between the last field and the Circle, and she suspects she knows which patron-family arranged it, but she will not put her name to a suspicion. She is looking for a traveler who can ride back along the convoy's last leg, find the weighing house or the swap point if one exists, and come back with something she can show the festival broker before the rotation closes. If she is right and cannot prove it, the Circle's honesty pretense frays. If she is wrong and says so, her own word frays instead.
