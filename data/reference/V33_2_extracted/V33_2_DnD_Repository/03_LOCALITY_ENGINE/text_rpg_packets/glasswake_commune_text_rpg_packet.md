# Glasswake Commune — Text RPG Flavor Packet

**Parent polity:** Sheresh Communes (Frigid Polar Zone)
**Type:** Research and containment commune
**Truth layer:** conservative_inference

## Atmosphere

The dome shows first across the snow — low, grey-white, the curve broken by the squat shapes of the containment buildings grafted onto its outer ring. Nothing about the approach is welcoming and nothing is decorative. The ward-paint on the outer panels is matte to keep glare down, and the observation towers along the perimeter are small and close-set, each with its own shuttered window. Cold light from the instruments bleeds out through the shutters at night and is visible from a long way across the ice.

The air inside the ring is thin and clean in a way that feels rehearsed. Staff move between buildings on roped walkways, and every rope has a tag on it — dated, initialed, the date of the next check already written. Nobody here argues with a protocol. They argue about a protocol, in writing, in their own time.

## Scene Openers

1. **Arrival, outer gate.** A commune screener reads a hand-held instrument across every person in the party and watches the needle without comment. The needle settles. The screener nods to an attendant, who writes three letters on a chit and hands it back. Until the chit is on the party's person, they are not cleared of the ice, only cleared of the gate.
2. **First morning, instrument hall.** A researcher has been at her bench since before the sun came up. She has not noticed the shift change outside the window. A junior technician is standing at the doorway with a second cup of hot broth and has been standing there for five minutes waiting for a gap in which to speak without interrupting the reading.
3. **Afternoon, quarantine corridor.** A cold-light experiment is running behind a sealed door. The corridor lamps dim on a predictable cycle as the instrument draws. Staff in the corridor time their sentences between the dimmings — they will simply stop mid-word when the light goes, and resume when it comes back.
4. **Late shift, shield bench.** Two shield technicians are repairing a panel that came in from an outer station. They are arguing about the panel's history — how many cycles it has seen, whether the etching is a fault or a design change — and they are arguing with references, pulling out slim field notebooks and reading aloud from them before they commit to a position.

## Social Register

- Precision is politeness. "The reading is unclear" is a complete sentence here, and "I don't know" is preferred over a guess.
- Submit to scanning without comment on first pass. Objecting to it marks a person as either unprepared or concealing, and both cost the same delay.
- Address researchers by their bench, not their seniority. "Cold-light bench" or "shield bench" is a polite opening; guessing someone's station wrong is a recoverable misstep.
- Do not touch an instrument. Do not lean on a bench. Do not step over a chalked line on a corridor floor — those lines are not decorative, and the person who drew them has already measured the room against them.
- Treat every breach as real until cleared. Skepticism is a private opinion here, not a public one.

## Signature Details

- **The ward-paint.** A matte grey-green applied in broad strokes across the outer panels of the dome and re-done on a posted schedule. Each re-paint carries a date-mark in the lower corner of the panel, and the technicians can tell a panel's history at a glance — whose crew last did it, whether the winter it went through was hard.
- **The rope tags.** Every walking rope between buildings has a cloth tag tied at the halfway point. Date of last inspection, initials of the inspector, date of next check. Nobody moves a tag. Nobody ties a new rope without one. A missing tag is a halted walkway until a technician can get to it.
- **The broth cart.** A wheeled cart that makes a slow circuit of the benches twice a shift. Hot broth, hard biscuit, nothing else. The operator knows which researchers will forget to eat if the cart does not stop at their bench, and the cart stops there anyway. It is the single most reliable institution in the commune.

## Complication Seed

A panel returned from an outer station is reading clean on the primary scan but dirty on a secondary test one of the shield technicians ran on her own initiative. She has not yet logged the secondary test, because logging it triggers a corridor-wide quarantine and the senior researcher on the cold-light bench has a visiting delegation arriving in two days. The technician is looking for a pair of outside hands — someone who can retrace the panel's field history, ideally by talking to the outer station's last handlers, and come back with enough to justify either logging the test or setting it aside. The window is before the delegation arrives, or before she loses the nerve to sit on her own data.
