# Whitebridge Commune — Text RPG Flavor Packet

**Parent polity:** Soreheim Alliance
**Type:** Crossing commune at a river span
**Truth layer:** conservative_inference

## Atmosphere

The bridge is what the settlement is built around and named for — a long low span of whitewashed stone crossing a river wide enough that the crossing takes a few minutes on foot. The commune buildings are arranged on both banks, with the main hall on the south approach and the waystations and supply sheds on the north, because the north side is where travelers coming off the bridge are more likely to stop and ask for help. The whitewash is re-done every year, communally, and the day it is re-done is a minor festival.

Wind off the river is constant. It smells of water and wet reed. Travelers moving through in weather learn to read the bridge's slick spots as quickly as the commune does, or they learn from a commune member who has stepped in to steady them without being asked. Help here is given before it is asked for, which means the commune's watch on the bridge is never off.

## Scene Openers

1. **Morning, south approach.** A traveler has pulled a cart to the lip of the bridge and is hesitating — the load is high and the wind is moving. A commune member is already walking up from the bridge house, unhurried, and will ask the traveler one question about the load before offering a helper at the lead horse for the crossing. The question and the offer are the same conversation here.
2. **Midday, bridge deck.** A clerk is standing at the center of the bridge with a slim book, logging the day's crossings — not as a tax, as a record. Travelers pass on either side of her and a few nod; she nods back and notes something without stopping the pen. The logging is hazard data. Commune members will read her book at the next meeting.
3. **Afternoon, north waystation.** A crew has come off a convoy run and is eating at the waystation's long table. A commune member sits with them and is listening more than talking — what the crew reports about the next leg is what the commune will tell the next traveler to head that direction. The crew knows this. They talk accordingly.
4. **Evening, whitewash storage.** A pair of commune members are checking the lime and brush stock at the storage shed against the schedule for the next whitewash day. The schedule is posted on the door in a hand that changes year to year because the task of keeping the schedule rotates. The conversation is about whether the river will be low enough on the posted day for scaffolding.

## Social Register

- Declare route intent on arrival. A traveler who says where they are coming from and where they are going gets a better set of warnings than a traveler who does not, and this is neither secret nor negotiable.
- Share hazard knowledge before departure. If you know something about the road you came from, tell the clerk at the bridge or the commune member at the waystation. This is the single strongest reputation-building act in the commune.
- Help is given, not requested. If a commune member has stepped in to help, accept the help and thank briefly. Insisting on paying for it shifts the register wrongly and will be declined.
- Do not run on the bridge. Do not lead a skittish animal across at a pace the deck will not tolerate. Do not pretend to know the crossing if you do not.
- Names are exchanged on the third meeting, not the first. "The clerk on the bridge today" is polite. Asking for her name on the first crossing is a small misstep.

## Signature Details

- **The whitewash.** Renewed every year on a posted day that shifts with the river's level. The bridge's white is visible from a long way off and is part of how travelers orient on the road. A weather-streaked bridge is an embarrassment the commune treats as a scheduling failure, not a cosmetic one.
- **The crossing book.** A slim cloth-bound ledger the clerk keeps on the bridge. Entries are short — direction, load type, weather notes, anything the traveler volunteered about the road behind them. The book is read aloud at the quarterly meeting and is the commune's working intelligence on the region.
- **The helper's hand.** There is a specific commune habit, taught young, of offering a hand at the lead of a horse or cart coming onto the bridge without making eye contact with the driver. The hand goes on the harness or the cart rail, the body stays out of the driver's line of sight, and the help continues until the crossing is clear. Commune children practice the motion on the school day the whitewash schedule is taught.

## Complication Seed

A traveler came off the bridge two days ago and gave the clerk a hazard report — a stretch of road south had a tree down across a ford, the traveler said, and the commune sent word along to the next two convoys to take the long route around. A convoy has now come through from that direction and says there is no tree, there never was a tree, and the long route cost them a full day. The clerk's book has the original report in it in her own hand. The commune member on the south approach suspects the original traveler was routing convoys for someone else's benefit, but the commune does not want to be the institution that stopped accepting hazard reports. The commune is looking for a pair of outside hands — someone who can find the original traveler if they are still in the region, or find out who benefited from the convoy diversion, and do it before the next meeting asks whether the book is still trustworthy. The window is a few days.
