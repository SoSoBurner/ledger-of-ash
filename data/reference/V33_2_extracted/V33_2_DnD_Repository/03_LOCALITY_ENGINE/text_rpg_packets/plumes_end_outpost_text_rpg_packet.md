# Plume's End Outpost — Text RPG Flavor Packet

**Parent polity:** Principality of Shelk (House Shelk)
**Type:** Northern road outpost
**Truth layer:** direct_canon

## Atmosphere

Plume's End is the last ordered thing on the road north before the country opens up. A stone-walled compound set against a bend in the highway: tavern to one side, armory and waystation clustered opposite, shrine at the back wall where the light catches it from the road. The yard in the middle is packed earth kept swept. Caravan wagons park in a rotation the Roadwardens enforce by eye, and by the time a traveler has crossed the yard to the tavern door, the wardens at the gate will have read the wagon's plates, the escort's plumes, and the cargo's seals.

Rumor cycles through here faster than people do. Every caravan that stops leaves a scrap of news behind — weather south of Shelkopolis, a delay on the Fairhaven leg, a sighting on the road that may or may not have been a celestial enforcer — and every caravan that leaves picks one up to take further north. The tavern room at Plume's End is where the day's road is written down in conversation before it is written down anywhere else.

## Scene Openers

1. **Dawn, gate check.** A caravan is being processed for the southbound run. Roadwardens are walking the wagons in pairs, one checking the seals on the cargo while the other talks to the drivers. A priest is at the shrine wall with a small tray of safe-journey offerings; the lead driver will visit the shrine before the first cart rolls, because that is how it is done here.
2. **Midmorning, tavern common room.** The tavern is half full — a mix of caravan staff eating late, Roadwardens off-shift, a traveling merchant writing in a ledger at the corner table. A rumor broker is making a slow circuit of the room, pausing at each table for the polite exchange of two sentences, leaving with one piece of news and arriving with another.
3. **Afternoon, waystation forge.** A caravan smith is repairing a cart axle that split on the last leg. His apprentice is running between the forge and the armory for a fitting the smith does not have in stock. Two drivers are squatting at the wall watching the repair, because their own schedules depend on whether the axle takes the afternoon or the whole day.
4. **Evening, shrine wall.** The last light is on the shrine. A Roadwarden has come off patrol with a report the captain will want to hear, but she stops at the shrine first. The offerings from the morning's caravans have been cleared; the evening's offerings have started to accumulate. She sets hers — a small coil of wax-cord from the tack of her horse — and goes on.

## Social Register

- State your business on approach. A driver at the gate who cannot say where he has come from or where he is going in one sentence is a driver the wardens will process slowly.
- Tavern-room rumor is traded, not given. If someone brings you news, you are expected to have news to trade. Not having any is fine, but then you listen and do not comment.
- Roadwardens are "warden" at the gate and "warden" at the tavern. A warden in the tavern is still on the clock in a sense Plume's End understands and outsiders sometimes do not.
- Respect the shrine. Do not walk past the wall without at least a glance in its direction. Offerings are small, practical, and personal; nothing flashy.
- Do not avoid scrutiny. Wardens remember a traveler who seemed to be ducking the gate check long after they have forgotten anyone who did not.

## Signature Details

- **The plume rack in the armory.** A wooden rack by the armory door where wardens hang their off-duty plumes during repair and cleaning. The rack is always staffed — a junior warden checking plumes in and out — and the rack is where a visitor learns at a glance how many wardens are on station today.
- **The shrine wall.** Rough stone, north-facing, with a weathered ledge where the day's offerings sit. Nothing elaborate — wax-cords, coins, small cloths, a dried flower. It is cleared by a junior clergy member at first light and last light, and the two clearings mark the outpost's informal day.
- **The tavern's slate.** A slate board by the kitchen door at the tavern, chalked with the current road warnings — weather, delays, a single line if something has been sighted on the road that wardens want travelers to know about. The slate is not official. The wardens let it stand because it saves them having to say aloud what the slate already says.

## Complication Seed

A northbound caravan has arrived at Plume's End a day late, with its lead escort missing and the remaining escort telling a story the drivers will not contradict aloud but will not confirm in private either. The patrol captain cannot detain the caravan on a driver's silence, and the merchants paying for the caravan want to move on at first light. A tavern rumor broker has already paid two of the drivers for what they would not say to the wardens, and she is looking for a traveler who can make the same run the caravan made, in reverse, fast enough to catch whatever was on the road before the trail goes cold. The window is the morning. The broker will pay in information, not coin.
