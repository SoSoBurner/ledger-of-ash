# V31 Canon-Safe Next Build Priority Analysis

V31 should be planned from the position that the package is already broadly playable at the world layer. The greatest remaining need is not broad new world lore. The greatest remaining need is deeper operational content that turns existing canon into a stronger play surface. The safest and highest-value work is therefore launch-surface deepening, continuity-surface filling, and crosslink strengthening around already asserted places, factions, institutions, route corridors, and live campaign state rather than ornamental worldspread.

## Primary conclusion
The next development lane should prioritize authored launch surfaces and continuity surfaces, not generalized world expansion. Existing canon already provides wide territorial spread, route identity, faction presence, institution presence, and named locality support. What still yields the highest return is the conversion of that asserted material into faster zero-to-scene packets, stronger campaign persistence, clearer faction handling, denser rumor routing, and more explicit offscreen consequence motion.

## 1. Highest-priority need: starter packets for surfaced localities
The highest-priority content lane remains starter support for surfaced localities that already exist as named, navigable world objects but still benefit most from stronger zero-to-scene packaging. The best canon-safe targets are:
- Arbiters Row
- Ashforge Citadel
- Ashforge Quarry
- Aurora Crown Commune
- Cosmoria
- Fairhaven
- Harvest Keep
- Hullwake Foundry
- Market Crossroads
- Mimolot Academy
- Panim Haven
- Shirshal
- Silent Haven
- Sunspire Haven
- Unity Square
- Witness Coil

These are high-value because they are already asserted locations and can be deepened without inventing unsupported canon. The correct design rule is to improve their play-entry stack, not to widen their lore footprint beyond what the package already supports.

Each locality should directly expose one clean start state: what the party sees on arrival, who matters immediately, what the opening pressure is, what can go wrong within ten minutes, where the party can go next, and what consequence begins moving if they delay. The target is instant table usability.

## 2. Next major need: campaign persistence content at the root
The live continuity layer is structurally present but still needs to be treated as a primary build surface instead of a maintenance afterthought. The root campaign persistence files are exactly the right scaffolding and should continue to be filled with validated play-derived entries:
- `../../CURRENT_CAMPAIGN_STATE.md`
- `../../CHANGED_LOCALITIES.md`
- `../../ACTIVE_FACTIONS.md`
- `../../UNRESOLVED_PRESSURES.md`
- `../../NPCS_THE_PARTY_DISTURBED.md`

This matters especially because the active campaign sheet already establishes a live scenario: House Shelk vs the Union, in Shelkopolis, with the party objective to find the daughter of Lord Myrs Shelk, plus named NPCs and active faction pressure. That means the repository is no longer only a lore surface. It is a continuity surface. The more faithfully these root files absorb real play, the more the package functions as a living campaign engine rather than a static archive.

## 3. Crosslink density is still too thin in the most table-critical places
Crosslink density should be strengthened so each starter locality directly points to:
- its arrival kit
- its escalation ladder
- its route card(s)
- its travel complication(s)
- 2 to 4 relevant named NPCs
- 1 active pressure
- 1 nearby fallback scene
- 1 consequence propagation note

This is canon-safe because it improves usability of asserted content rather than inventing new truth layers. The repository already contains many of these surfaces separately. The remaining work is to make them converge cleanly on a DM’s first-use path so a locality can be run from one card stack instead of inferred from multiple browse paths.

## 4. Full faction-primer parity remains a major value lane
Full faction-primer parity is still needed for the factions most likely to enter immediate play, public rumor, negotiation, coercion, or consequence routing. The highest-value targets are:
- House Shelk
- The Union
- Roadwardens Order
- Red Hood Guild
- Great House Citadel Circle
- Magi Magistratus
- Sheresh Dome Stewards
- Agricultural Syndicate
- Engineers Consortium
- house-level remnant claimants and enforcement bodies where already asserted

Each primer should state plainly what the faction wants, how it operates, what it fears, what it is doing now, and how it reacts to party interference. The design goal is not abstract lore description. The design goal is immediate operational clarity under pressure.

## 5. Rumor starter expansion is still one of the safest high-return additions
Rumor starter expansion remains needed by polity and by route, especially across locality clusters and major road or harbor corridors where travel, trade, suspicion, or tension matter. Rumors are high-value because they activate existing canon without forcing hard retcons. They let asserted places, factions, and pressures begin speaking to one another in play before the party physically reaches every surface.

The best rumor work is corridor-aware rather than generic. It should answer: what are people saying here right now, who benefits from that rumor, what does it misdirect away from, and which nearby locality or institution becomes the next logical scene if the party follows it.

## 6. Consequence propagation and offscreen world motion remain underdeveloped
Consequence propagation is still the clearest line between a package that stores content and a package that runs a campaign world. Offscreen progression should explicitly track:
- which faction benefits if the party delays
- what changes in markets, registries, communes, quarries, or courts after incidents
- which NPCs become more hostile, frightened, or opportunistic
- which roads become safer or more dangerous over time

This is what turns the package from a static archive into a living campaign engine. Consequences should not only record what the party did. They should move pressure outward into institutions, movement lanes, witness behavior, and public interpretation.

## 7. More authored inside packets are needed for civic institutions
More authored inside packets are still needed for the civic and operational institutions players are most likely to walk into mid-session, especially:
- registries
- courts
- guild halls
- shrines and temples
- quarries and foundries
- estate compounds
- checkpoints and harbormaster spaces

These are high-value because they support immediate play when characters walk into meaningful institutions. The package is strongest when it can answer not only what a place is, but what it feels like to enter, who controls pace there, what the first social obstacle is, and what behavior marks someone as out of place.

The live House Shelk campaign should continue receiving direct packet support because the current runtime sheet already justifies it. The most immediately useful canon-safe additions are:

- a Find the Daughter of Lord Myrs Shelk clue ladder
- a House Shelk vs Union escalation track
- NPC cards for:
  - Lord Myrs Shelk
  - Sir Harold
  - Sir Hendrick
  - Ronald Tulk
  - Rodge Holt
  - General Ethic
  - Rem Tolk
  - High Priest Smog Lengle

These items are justified by the active session sheet rather than speculative invention. The governing rule is to keep each of these packets focused on current-campaign utility: access control, witness posture, pressure routing, clue containment, escalation consequences, and promotion conditions for stronger continuity.

## Antagonist dossier integration value
The uploaded antagonist dossier adds a high-density adversarial reference surface that strengthens V31 without requiring unsupported canon expansion. Its strongest use in the next build cycle is not as a replacement for locality or continuity work, but as a supporting pressure lens for faction behavior, social threat tone, and GM-facing opposition handling. Integrated correctly, it increases deployment readiness for hostile or coercive actors while preserving the package’s repository-governed canon rules.

## Final conclusion
V31 confirms that the next highest-value work is more authored launch surfaces and continuity surfaces, not more generalized worldspread. The safest and most valuable additions remain:
- more starter packets
- filled campaign persistence files
- stronger locality-to-route-to-pressure crosslinks
- more faction primers
- more rumor packs
- more offscreen consequence tracking
- current-campaign packets for House Shelk vs the Union

All recommendations should continue to follow the same governing constraints: treat the package as standalone and repository-governed, preserve asserted canon exactly, do not invent unsupported facts as if established, favor play usefulness, continuity stability, and retrieval reliability over ornamental expansion, and frame all additions as canon-safe deepenings of already asserted world structures, places, factions, institutions, pressures, and campaign-state material.
