# Calendar Crosswalk Guide

This guide ties festivals, ritual cadence, harvest windows, route stress, bargaining cycles, and hazard seasons together so the world behaves like a civilization instead of a stack of place files.

## Seasonal operating rhythm

### Late thaw and first movement
- Dome and mountain polities reopen fragile corridors.
- Survey, repair, and escort contracts surge before full trade volume returns.
- Records offices see a rise in petitions delayed through harsher seasons.
- Best for: first-contact diplomacy, inspection drama, route reopening, and old obligations coming due.

### High harvest and civic counting season
- Sunspire Haven, Harvest Circle, Canal Thorn Exchange, and grain-linked nodes become politically sensitive.
- Registry pressure rises because abundance changes inheritance, debt, tariffs, and household bargaining.
- Best for: food politics, convoy protection, hoarding rumors, and lawful extortion.

### Festival and prestige season
- Courts, academies, and great houses become vulnerable to scandal because visitors multiply and status must be performed.
- Magical items, ceremonial textiles, copied texts, and bonded luxury cargo move more openly.
- Best for: elite intrigue, symbolic insult, theft under etiquette, and rumor escalation.

### Storm, whiteout, and hardening season
- Freight routes narrow to the dependable few.
- Roadwardens, dome stewards, harbor authorities, and house quartermasters become more powerful.
- Best for: survival pressure, emergency authority, convoy scarcity, and social claustrophobia.

## Domain crosswalk

| Domain | What changes in season | GM use |
|---|---|---|
| Trade | Goods shift from luxury circulation to survival movement | Turn prices, escorts, and priorities into scene pressure |
| Religion | Mourning, blessing, oath, and passage rites become more visible under strain | Give choices moral weight without forcing doctrine dumps |
| Magic | Accepted applications narrow toward utility and safety in hard seasons | Make flashy casting socially expensive in the wrong place |
| Rumor | Scarcity and prestige seasons both accelerate rumor spread | Escalate false certainty before actual proof |
| Law | More exceptions, emergency procedure, and document scrutiny appear | Create procedural friction that feels world-native |

## Timing principles for play
- Use calendars to explain why a place is tense *now*.
- Let ritual and labor cycles visibly alter traffic, mood, and available NPCs.
- Tie one pressure in each starter packet to a calendar condition whenever possible.
