35 one-page antagonists + hierarchy maps + GM overview + the “third axis” of setting-defining evil

Grounding rule: names, public roles, settlements, institutions, and polity logic are drawn from the uploaded V30_8 repository. # GM-Facing Overview

This packet is built to separate canon-safe public truth from GM-facing hidden structure. The five absolute monsters are terrifying not only because they are vile, but because they are strong enough to act, embedded enough to endure, and useful enough that society helps hide them.

Source of power: why they are formidable even before the reveal. Cultural wound: what secondary behaviors they produce in the world around them. |

Sheresh rewards cold competence, route truth, and survival discipline. Its hidden evil survives by posing as indispensable technical or procedural necessity.

| Hierarchy | Key chain | GM meaning |

| Arcane survival | Talar -> Pella | Turns magical maintenance into structural authority. |

Supplement: The Five as Catastrophes with Appetites

This supplement changes nothing in the existing packet. It only adds a mythic-abomination layer for the five absolute monsters.

They need to feel less like “extremely bad people” and more like catastrophes with appetites.

Not merely intimate. Not merely cruel. Not merely traumatic.

They should feel operatic, blasphemous, world-souring, and spiritually excessive.

The most vile and grandiose villains do not just harm people. They make claims like this with their actions:

- love is a joke

- mercy is a flaw

- innocence exists to be defiled

- dignity is a temporary costume

- the sacred was made to be profaned

- hope is sweetest when extinguished publicly

- the world is most honest when it is screaming

So the five top villains should not feel like dangerous rulers or predators. They should feel like dark principles wearing names.

Each one should be the kind of figure people build taboos around. Mothers should use them as warnings. Priests should go quiet at their mention. Hardened soldiers should spit after saying their names, as if language itself feels dirtied.

They should each carry a sense of mythic violation.

Not: “I will kill you.” But: “I will make your gods watch.”

Not: “I will conquer this city.” But: “I will teach this city to kneel to its own humiliation.”

Not: “I will burn the temple.” But: “I will turn prayer into smoke and make the faithful breathe it.”

That is vile and grandiose.

Supplemental Addendum — Kordr Vulkhand

Kordr Vulkhand — The Desecrator of Valor

Kordr should not feel like a berserker. He should feel like a war-revelation. The nightmare at the end of every soldier’s prayer. The thing that arrives when courage itself is being put on trial.

He does not simply kill the brave. He exists to prove bravery obscene.

He should believe that every vow of protection is a lie waiting to be split open. Every oath is vanity. Every heroic stand is merely a prettier form of begging. He wants the world to learn that the strong are only the weak who have not yet been properly introduced to terror.

So he does not seek victory alone. Victory is beneath him. He seeks public unmaking.

He wants citadels to fall while their defenders are still shouting promises. He wants champions dragged through the blood of those they failed. He wants children to watch their fathers lose. He wants lovers forced to choose which scream to answer. He wants guardians to die hearing the names of the people they swore would never be touched.
