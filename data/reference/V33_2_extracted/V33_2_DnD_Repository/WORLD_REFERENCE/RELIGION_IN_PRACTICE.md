# Religion in Practice

## Major pattern
Religions in this world are not just temple labels. They are public behavioral systems that explain suffering, justify law, shape daily ritual, and determine what kinds of power look respectable.

## Examples by social function
- **Knowledge** faiths preserve records, sponsor libraries, charge entry, and treat observation as duty.
- **Compassion** faiths form comfort circles and empathy rituals; suffering is shared so it becomes socially bearable.
- **Fear** faiths frame doomsday as warning and future corruption as threat; talismans, omens, and prophecy become civic behaviors.
- **Joy** faiths normalize euphoric ceremony, memory-lightening, revelry, and soft denial of existential burden.
- **Ambition** faiths sanctify sacrifice, upward striving, magical advancement, and hierarchy.
- **Contemplation** faiths build cultures of meditation, abstraction, philosophy, and dangerous overthinking.

## Religion and polity
Religious life often blends with civic authority. In the Principalities, belief intersects with archives, noble education, sacrifice economies, magical policing, and burial systems. In Sheresh, magical daily life softens the line between artistry and devotion. In Soreheim, doctrine tends to justify function, discipline, and collective necessity.

## Table rule
Whenever players ask what ordinary people believe, answer at three layers:
1. what the temple says
2. what the polity rewards
3. what common people quietly do to survive
