# Current Campaign State

## Core instability
House Shelk needs its daughter found before the search becomes a public test of legitimacy. The Union does not need to cause the disappearance to profit from it; it only needs the search to become visible, procedural, and expensive.

## What is happening right now
- The Golden Crew is in **Shelkopolis** during **thunder storms** on **day 30**.
- The party is operating while carrying a **Wanted** criminality flag.
- The opening point of contact is the **General Store**, which may be a witness site, bait site, rumor trap, or neutral-feeling surface that is no longer neutral.

## Why the opening matters
This is not only a missing-person problem. It is a race between:
1. private recovery
2. public embarrassment
3. factional narrative capture
4. procedural containment that makes every next question costlier

## The most unstable truths
- Someone inside or near House Shelk already fears blame more than they fear the daughter remaining missing.
- Storm conditions distort messenger timing, route confidence, and witness certainty.
- The party's wanted status means any official help creates a second problem.
- The Union can weaponize delay even without direct involvement.

## What each side wants
### House Shelk
A fast, quiet solution that preserves dignity and keeps servants, retainers, and roadwardens from improvising their own stories.

### The Union
A visible mishandling, overreach, false accusation, or road-level inconvenience that can be reframed as proof House Shelk no longer governs cleanly.

### The party
To locate the daughter, survive the city's scrutiny, and avoid becoming the easiest explanation for every new irregularity.

## Likely next developments
- A witness goes cautious or disappears behind procedure.
- A House retainer tries to steer inquiry away from one socially dangerous angle.
- A Union-adjacent broker offers help that creates debt.
- The storm opens one narrow travel window and closes two easier ones.

## Best next scenes
1. Quiet questioning at the General Store while everyone pretends it is ordinary commerce.
2. A Shelk contact forcing the party to choose between speed and cover.
3. A roadwarden encounter where papers matter more than truth.
4. A servant or family retainer revealing that household shame and household fear are not the same thing.
