# Systemic Reveal Guide: House Shelk vs the Union

Use this guide to make the opening campaign reveal the larger world rather than feeling like a sealed local incident.

## Conflict spine
A missing daughter in Shelkopolis is not only a household emergency. It is also:
- a **house legitimacy crisis** in a polity where public harmony is political capital
- a **route-control crisis** because House Shelk's authority depends on secure and trusted movement
- a **Union opportunity** because procedural delay and commercial interpretation weaken hereditary command
- a **religious framing contest** because fear, compassion, knowledge, and renewal each produce different public stories
- a **class-safety problem** because witnesses know being correct too early can still destroy them

## What each scene should reveal

### 1. Public contact surface
Reveal that ordinary civilians do not speak freely because witness testimony is politically expensive.

### 2. House contact surface
Reveal that House Shelk cares about sequence, sponsorship, and embarrassment control as much as retrieval.

### 3. Union-adjacent contact surface
Reveal that a trade network can weaponize delay, paperwork, and rumor without drawing a sword.

### 4. Religious or omen surface
Reveal that the same facts become different social realities depending on which faith language reaches the crowd first.

### 5. Route or movement surface
Reveal that where the daughter moved matters because roads, gates, guild halls, and patrol visibility determine who can confirm anything cleanly.

## Why this is happening now
- House authority is already vulnerable to interpretation pressure from Union-style procedure.
- Shelk's prestige culture makes visible scandal disproportionately costly.
- Thunderstorm conditions distort witness confidence, route timing, and rumor certainty.
- Wanted-status adventurers intensify the legitimacy question around who is authorized to investigate.

## Consequence mapping

| If players prioritize... | Immediate gain | Hidden cost | System revealed |
|---|---|---|---|
| Speed | Earlier clues | sloppier legitimacy | route power and witness fragility |
| House loyalty | cleaner access to noble surfaces | Union suspicion and civilian hesitation | noble protocol as statecraft |
| Public openness | broader rumor intake | narrative loss of control | class fear and factional interpretation |
| Religious mediation | calmer crowds or sanctuary | evidence becomes morally pre-framed | faith as social infrastructure |
| Union negotiation | market access and alternate informants | House distrust and long-tail obligations | logistics as politics |

## GM reminder
Every answer in this campaign should connect upward:
local clue -> institutional interest -> regional pressure -> Doomsday-shaped material condition or divine logic
