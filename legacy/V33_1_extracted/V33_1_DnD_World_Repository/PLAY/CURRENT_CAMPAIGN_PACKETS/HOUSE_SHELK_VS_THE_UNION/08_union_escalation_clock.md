# Union Escalation Clock

Use this when the party hesitates, asks for official help, or lets witnesses begin comparing stories.

## Clock 1: Quiet commercial pressure
**Trigger:** The party asks public questions in the wrong place or repeats House Shelk names too freely.

**Signs:**
- inventory disappears from one useful shop
- one messenger becomes suddenly unavailable
- an innkeeper becomes polite in a way that means they were warned

## Clock 2: Narrative capture
**Trigger:** The Union can describe the problem before House Shelk can define it.

**Signs:**
- helpful strangers begin using the same phrasing
- a broker offers help with terms that make the party look dependent
- a witness repeats an interpretation instead of an observation

## Clock 3: Procedural enclosure
**Trigger:** The party touches officials while carrying wanted-status risk.

**Signs:**
- roadwardens ask for names before facts
- a clerk requests a delay that sounds routine but changes who controls the file
- a House retainer asks the party not to speak to one specific office

## Clock 4: Expensive rescue
**Trigger:** House Shelk chooses speed through prestige instead of quiet competence.

**Signs:**
- visible guards appear where servants should have sufficed
- rumor turns from concern to spectacle
- the daughter becomes politically harder to return even if physically located

## GM use
Advance one stage whenever the party trades secrecy for convenience. If they move fast, stay private, and verify witnesses before speaking upward, reduce one stage or delay the next one.
