# Find the Daughter of Lord Myrs Shelk Clue Ladder

This ladder is intentionally built from currently asserted runtime surfaces, named live NPCs, and already surfaced Shelkopolis canon. It is designed to help the DM move the investigation without inventing unsupported hard truth too early.

## Node 1: House request and containment
- **Surface:** Lord Myrs Shelk or his gatekeeping circle
- **What this provides:** Why discretion matters and who is allowed to ask questions
- **Failure cost:** The search becomes visible faction theater

## Node 2: Last reliable domestic witness
- **Surface:** Sir Harold, Sir Hendrick, or household retainers
- **What this provides:** Last known routine, household fracture, or protected timeline gap
- **Failure cost:** Witnesses begin aligning statements instead of remembering

## Node 3: Public contact point
- **Surface:** General Store, market-side staff, or clerk-facing witnesses in Shelkopolis
- **What this provides:** A sighting, purchase, message, or directional lead
- **Failure cost:** The public layer starts producing rumor instead of memory

## Node 4: Religious or confessional lane
- **Surface:** High Priest Smog Lengle or another sacred testimony channel
- **What this provides:** Private concern, confession trace, omen interpretation, or protected contact path
- **Failure cost:** Spiritual counsel becomes sealed or politically managed

## Node 5: Union leverage surface
- **Surface:** The Union side of the campaign pressure
- **What this provides:** Evidence that the disappearance is becoming useful to someone outside House Shelk
- **Failure cost:** The party learns too late that the search was being narrated by an opponent

## Ladder rule
Every time the party delays, misspeaks publicly, or forces access, promote one of these:
- witness confidence drops
- the Union gains narrative ground
- House Shelk narrows access
- a neutral contact becomes factionally tagged
