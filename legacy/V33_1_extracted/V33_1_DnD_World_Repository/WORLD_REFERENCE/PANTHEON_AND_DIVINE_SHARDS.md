# Pantheon and Divine Shards

## Core model
The world begins with the One, an all-powerful being of the Astral Sea, who split reality by creating the material plane. That act divided aspects of the One into a pantheon of twenty shards. Each god is understood through three parts: divine body or shard, consciousness, and spirit-character. This is the metaphysical root of divine conflict, mortal religion, and the fear that identity itself can be destroyed or absorbed.

## High-impact divine powers
- **Desperation** seeks recombination at any cost and caused the world-breaking doomsday event by forcing too much divinity through the material barrier.
- **Destruction** and **Agony** contain Desperation from the lower realms while also preying on mortal worlds through abyssal and infernal structures.
- **Ambition** is the major hidden corrupter among the gods, using erased identity, stolen shards, and manufactured forgetting to operate behind false histories.
- **Knowledge** preserves records, truth, and libraries, but is itself vulnerable to missing information and manipulated memory.
- **Creation** keeps altering the world through dragon creation, shadow experimentation, and cosmological engineering.

## Why this matters in play
Divinity in this setting is not distant wallpaper. Gods shape:
- religious institutions
- political doctrine
- local law and custom
- doomsday interpretation
- planar hazards
- what mortals believe is possible

## GM use
When a scene touches prophecy, recordkeeping, corruption, magical pollution, undead labor, ambition, or ritual authority, assume a divine fingerprint is somewhere behind the local explanation.
