# V32_4 World Interconnection Work Specs

V32_4 defines the next build program as an interconnection architecture pass. The objective is to make the setting feel connected, immersive, alive, real, natural, and high fantasy by increasing causal density between existing canon layers rather than adding broad disconnected lore. This build treats the setting as already broad at the world layer and identifies the primary remaining need as operational interdependence between cosmology, geography, law, religion, economy, institutions, routes, hazards, settlements, factions, NPCs, and campaign persistence.

## Executive diagnosis
The setting already contains strong cosmology, differentiated polities, route structure, legal identities, hazard identities, institutional roles, religious pressures, settlement anchors, and runtime consequence expectations. The thin area is not top-layer breadth. The thin area is connective depth. Important entities exist, but too many of them can still be read in isolation rather than as parts of a living system. The highest-value work is therefore to specify how major world elements materially sustain one another, politically constrain one another, mythically interpret one another, and produce downstream table consequences.

## Governing framework
Use the following interconnection rule across all future content generation and reconciliation work:

1. Every major canon element must connect upward to cosmology, catastrophe history, or deep myth.
2. Every major canon element must connect sideways to at least two institutions, factions, routes, or neighboring systems.
3. Every major canon element must connect downward to ordinary life, material culture, public behavior, labor, law, or household practice.
4. Every major canon element must connect forward to campaign consequences, rumor movement, market change, civic stress, or route condition shifts.
5. No major polity, institution, route, hazard, settlement, or named office may remain isolated.
6. No new content may rely on outside intellectual property, generic fantasy filler, or unsupported external assumptions.

## Polity integration specs
### The Principalities
Build the Principalities as a road-governed, archive-aware, tax-sensitive, guild-competed civilization where public order, noble display, archival authority, logistics, and private coercion visibly overlap. Geography must create politics through roads, ports, taxable crossings, estate management, and chokepoints. Each principality must be legible through what the crown assigned it to solve and what material systems it now depends on to preserve relevance. Road policing, archive custody, prison labor, noble education, raw-material production, and union-mediated trade must visibly affect daily life, clothing, contracts, traffic, and fear.

Required work:
- Define how each principality exports dependence into every other one.
- Define which guild coalitions weaken or bypass each house authority.
- Define how local law differs at roads, estates, markets, docks, prisons, archives, and temple-interfacing spaces.
- Define what ordinary people believe each house is for, what elites know, and what rival houses exploit.
- Define region-native prestige goods, uniforms, seals, passes, route papers, arrest forms, tax records, and social markers.

### Sheresh
Build Sheresh as a survival-engine civilization where art, ritual, mutation, magical labor, shielding, purification, and dome governance are structurally linked. The polity must feel like a place where magical abundance is inseparable from contamination management. Private property, exile, cantrip-normal daily life, and aesthetic expression must all be downstream of exposure risk, enclosure systems, route readability, and engineered civic maintenance.

Required work:
- Connect dome stability, off-grid risk, mutation onset management, labor stratification, and local legal enforcement.
- Specify how mutation shapes profession, stigma, prestige, medicine, romance, inheritance, and off-grid economies.
- Specify how ritual art, music, warding, purification, and civic ceremony overlap in visible public life.
- Specify what breaks first when shielding, reactor support, transport, or archive truth fails.

### Soreheim
Build Soreheim as a tower-alliance civilization where architecture, vertical logistics, bridge rotation, production quotas, caste function, giant administration, and wasteland predation all visibly shape social order. The polity must feel like a place where engineering is politics and where survival depends on interdependent labor strata.

Required work:
- Connect tower type, bowl districts, bridge access, production floors, livestock systems, and giant governance roles.
- Specify how labor assignment, food access, ownership, and movement rights change by tower layer and guild dependence.
- Specify how predator pressure, unfinished towers, and supply interruption alter urban behavior and military posture.
- Specify how scientific progress, state expectation, and social value are made visible in ordinary life.

### Psanan
Build Psanan as an ash-and-forge polity where matriarchal domestic law, blood tradition, extraction, dueling, dangerous forests, and war-readiness are materially linked. The polity must feel like a place where home sovereignty and external danger shape every social expectation.

Required work:
- Connect household law, wartime authority transfer, marriage gifts, blood vows, mining, hunting, and beast-handling.
- Specify how hardness-based value systems change coin logic, craft prestige, legal seriousness, and trade negotiation.
- Specify how forests, extraction risks, and martial identity shape architecture, travel, inheritance, and public challenge culture.

### Nomdara
Build Nomdara as the moving connective tissue of the setting rather than only a caravan. It must function as translator, distributor, price manipulator, rumor carrier, diplomatic buffer, and contract synchronizer between incompatible civilizations.

Required work:
- Define how Nomdara arbitrages differences in calendars, law, currencies, route dangers, and taboo goods.
- Define what categories of goods only move because Nomdara can move them.
- Define how purchase strikes, hospitality codes, and convoy protection change regional politics.
- Define how Nomdara becomes the world’s fastest rumor layer without becoming an omniscient neutral actor.

## Institutional crosslink specs
Every major institution must be specified through the following fields: jurisdiction, funding base, public role, hidden role, dependencies, rivalries, records produced, failure consequences, and table-use value.

Priority institutions to harden:
- roadwardens and all road-policing derivatives
- archive and treasury authorities
- magic investigators and magistrate structures
- prison and labor-custody systems
- education and magic-school authorities
- guild coalitions and trade mediators
- dome, shield, or reactor-facing Sheresh maintenance bodies
- Soreheim tower management and allocation offices
- funerary or afterlife-purchase systems
- Nomdaran convoy councils and mobile adjudication circles

For each institution, add:
- one commoner-facing scene type
- one elite-facing scene type
- one route-facing interaction
- one religious or moral tension
- one failure cascade affecting at least two other systems

## Calendar and chronology specs
Timekeeping must become an active interconnection layer. Build a formal cross-calendar program covering contract notation, tax due dates, convoy timing, ritual observance, route seasonality, labor rotation, trial scheduling, and archival dating.

Required work:
- Create a cross-calendar conversion framework for all major polities.
- Define which institutions require dual-dated records.
- Define what kinds of disputes arise from incompatible calendars.
- Connect doomsday counting, seasonal danger windows, festival timing, and convoy route availability.
- Define how commoners, elites, and mobile traders each reference time differently.

## Trade, route, and material culture specs
Trade must function as the bloodstream of the setting. Every major good should have an origin, transport path, restriction profile, prestige profile, ritual use, black-market relevance, and replacement scarcity.

Required work:
- Build route-and-goods matrices for staple goods, luxury goods, magical maintenance goods, funerary goods, archive goods, weapons, prestige textiles, anti-magic devices, and contamination-control tools.
- Define chokepoints, inspections, tolls, route hazards, and substitute paths.
- Define which goods reveal polity identity on sight.
- Define region-native tools, office markers, household objects, and ceremonial items.
- Define how law, weather, hazard state, and faction pressure change route value.

## Religion-to-daily-life specs
Religious influence must alter the lived world. For each major religious force, specify impacts on mourning, hospitality, punishment, education, architecture, festival behavior, legal tone, emotional norms, and disaster interpretation.

Required work:
- Produce polity-by-polity public behavior deltas by major religious influence.
- Define how sermons, offerings, rites, comfort circles, prophecy, archive practice, ecstatic ritual, and competitive blessing affect ordinary schedules and expectations.
- Define how shared religion does not erase regional difference.
- Define how religion changes public responses to catastrophe, mutation, magic, crime, scarcity, and social failure.

## Rumor, record, and hidden truth specs
Truth must be unevenly distributed. For every major topic, use four truth layers: common belief, elite belief, institutional record, hidden truth.

Required work:
- Build rumor ladders for major cosmological, political, and route-sensitive topics.
- Assign which classes, professions, and regions have access to which truth layer.
- Define how archives, guilds, clergy, smugglers, magistrates, and caravans distort or preserve information.
- Define the social risk of repeating each kind of claim in each region.

## Hazard-to-culture specs
Hazards must shape culture continuously rather than only at encounter scale.

Required work for each hazard family:
- define architectural adaptation
- define drills and civic routines
- define slang and superstition
- define childhood preparation
- define labor effects
- define compensation, insurance, or loss-sharing logic
- define prestige and stigma patterns
- define route warning practices

Priority hazard families:
- magical contamination and mutation risk
- route corruption and unsafe crossings
- polar or axial instability effects
- storm-sea and whirlpool systems
- tower-wasteland predation
- extraction and forge-zone danger
- prison, bail, and coerced labor risk where law itself acts as an environmental pressure

## Settlement crosslink specs
Every major locality must be legible as part of a larger system. Each settlement packet should include:
- larger route role
- institutional jurisdiction
- key exports and dependencies
- local hazard profile
- religious tone in public life
- rumor environment
- legal friction points
- current pressure state
- named offices and who matters there
- table-use surfaces for arrival, negotiation, investigation, conflict, downtime, and consequence tracking

## NPC and office network specs
Named NPCs and office roles must become networked rather than isolated. Each important NPC or officeholder should carry explicit links to:
- home polity and locality
- institution or faction affiliation
- authority scope
- dependencies and rivals
- rumor relevance
- route relevance
- current pressure exposure
- likely reaction to player interference

At minimum, every major locality should expose one administrator, one enforcer, one broker, one ritual or moral authority, and one pressure-bearing nonelite.

## Campaign consequence persistence specs
Session actions must be able to update the living world without overwriting baseline canon. All future campaign-state work should record consequences in the following categories:
- NPC status changes
- institution posture changes
- route safety changes
- market availability changes
- rumor spread changes
- legal enforcement intensity changes
- local morale or fear changes
- hazard-state changes
- archive access or archive loss changes
- faction alignment or hostility changes

Each consequence entry should state scope, duration, likely propagation targets, and promotion rules into wider world state.

## Validation criteria
The interconnection pass succeeds only if all of the following become true:
- no major polity reads as flavor-isolated
- every major institution has visible commoner, elite, and route-facing relevance
- every major settlement can be explained through larger systems rather than local description alone
- calendars, trade, rumor, religion, and hazard visibly affect one another
- NPCs and offices are embedded in system networks
- campaign actions can generate recorded, propagating world effects
- region-native goods, tools, and rites make different places feel materially distinct
- the setting feels more alive because existing canon now changes other canon when pressured
