# Universe Primer

This guide is a derived, player-safe and GM-usable summary subordinate to structured canon. It is built from the current repository’s cosmology, polity, route, locality, and pantheon layers.

## Core cosmology
The Material Planet is the central inhabited world. It sits inside a larger cosmological structure shaped by the Astral Sky, the Ethereal Plane, Limbo in the world’s core, and the post-Doomsday influence of elemental planetary forces. Doomsday fractured older divine and elemental equilibrium, leaving scars such as the Mother Storm and Umbra and creating the unstable but durable current age.

The Elemental Planets of Fire, Ocean, Wind, and Rock do not read as decorative astronomy. They regulate day and night, weather, tides, stability, and ongoing magical pressure. The Material Planet flips on its axis in recurring cycles, so calendar, travel, ritual, agriculture, and governance all inherit cosmological consequences rather than merely local ones.

## Divine structure
The pantheon emerges from the fragmentation of the Primordial Unity. Divine contradiction is preserved as canon where the corpus requires it, especially around Vonalzo, Remeny, seeds, memory loss, and manipulated divine history. Worship on the Material Planet is not abstract: it shapes law, ritual, fear, trade, mourning, contemplation, ambition, renewal, and public expectations.

## Major civilizations
- **The Principalities**: noble, legal, commercial, ritual, and espionage-heavy polity-complex with strong internal diversity.
- **Shelk**: fashion, road security, noble decorum, cultural refinement, and visibly integrated devotion.
- **Roaz**: legality, industry, enforcement, antimagic posture, and disciplined civic identity.
- **Panim**: funerary mediation, afterlife commerce, consequence, rite, and grave public trust.
- **Mimolot**: scholarship, magical knowledge control, education, and access politics.
- **Cosmouth**: maritime commerce, archives, shipbuilding, and port pragmatism.
- **Zootia**: raw material reliability, agricultural support, and practical seasonal logistics.
- **Shirsh**: magical investigation, controlled inquiry, and regulation.
- **The Union**: mediation, guild arbitration, exchange, and precedent.
- **Soreheim Alliance**: giant-led quota civilization of towers, production, communal ambition, and industrial discipline.
- **Sheresh Communes**: dome-protected magical societies shaped by creativity, contamination awareness, and seasonal identity.
- **Psanan**: volcanic matriarchal forge polity of endurance, descent, house authority, and controlled trade windows.
- **Nomdara Caravan**: mobile trade civilization reading the world through routes, timing, and negotiated passage.

## Present-age reading
The world’s most important tensions are not only wars or apocalypses. They are lived pressures: route insecurity, noble rivalry, ritual legitimacy, magical contamination, industrial quota strain, volcanic hazard, harvest and supply dependency, and the constant effort to keep institutions credible under stress.
