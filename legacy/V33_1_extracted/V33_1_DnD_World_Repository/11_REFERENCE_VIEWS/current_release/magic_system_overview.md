# Magic System Overview

V33_1 integrates magic into the world setting as a **post-Doomsday, jurisdiction-sensitive reality-override system** rather than as a generic fantasy spell list.

## Core rule
Magic only makes sense when all of the following are considered together:
- cosmological substrate
- user access
- authorization and legitimacy
- resource burden
- execution channels
- local validation conditions
- interference and enforcement
- world consequence

## What magic is in this setting
Magic is the controlled imposition of an artificial pattern onto reality. It can alter matter, force, cognition, memory, spirit-state, concealment, oath legitimacy, sanctity, motion, thresholds, and symbolic value, but it always does so through cost, structure, and consequence.

## Why this world does not treat magic as generic
The Material Planet is a **broken but stabilized world**. Doomsday ruptured the world. The elemental planets now help hold it in working order. The Astral Sky, Ethereal Plane, Limbo, Umbra, Mother Storm, contamination zones, and glyph-corrupted sites all continue to shape magical permissiveness and danger.

That means magic is always influenced by:
- region
- institution
- law
- cultic interpretation
- site condition
- environmental pressure
- public fear
- available materials and sanctioned process

## The main practical layers
### 1. Access
Who can do magic at all.

### 2. Authorization
Whether that act is recognized as lawful, sacred, licensed, tolerated, or criminal.

### 3. Resource
What powers the act: body, ritual preparation, relic, site, office, or group burden.

### 4. Execution
How the act is performed: voice, gesture, materials, spatial relation, witness, or apparatus.

### 5. Resolution
Whether the act lands automatically, is contested, is resisted, is degraded, or fails.

### 6. Persistence
What has to keep carrying the burden after the first moment of casting.

### 7. Interference
How the act is detected, dispelled, suppressed, or legally escalated.

### 8. Consequence
What changes afterward in memory, politics, legitimacy, sanctity, archive power, or future stability.

## Cost rule
Magic never produces consequence-free miracles. Even clean success may still create:
- bodily strain
- memory shear
- contamination uptake
- visible trace
- relic wear
- sanctity shifts
- legal exposure
- public panic
- archive evidence
- stronger enforcement pressure later

## Execution rule
Magic may require one or more channels:
- spoken formula, chant, oath, or prayer
- trained gesture or body-memory
- inks, fibers, metals, glass, relic matter, or consumed catalysts
- correct spatial alignment
- a chamber, shrine, forge, estate, dome, archive, or bastion
- witness validation for high-risk or legitimacy-bearing rites

## Why non-damage magic matters so much here
Many of the setting’s most dangerous magical acts do not mainly injure the body. They:
- expose lies
- break concealment
- mark people for authorities
- collapse legitimacy
- bind promises
- alter memory credibility
- render a site ritually unsafe
- deny passage, access, or sanctuary

## Use at the table
Ask these in order:
1. What source of magic is this?
2. Is it lawful, sacred, licensed, tolerated, or illicit here?
3. What pays the burden?
4. What channel or apparatus is required?
5. What local condition helps or hurts it?
6. Who notices if it succeeds or fails?
7. What changes afterward besides hit points?
