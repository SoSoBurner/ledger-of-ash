# Shelkopolis–Fairhaven Corridor Route Ecology Packet

**Truth layer:** conservative inference
**Hierarchy:** Material Planet > Principality of Shelk
**Corridor nodes:** Shelkopolis, Fairhaven, Plume's End Outpost

**Road or lane condition:** The corridor is usable, but its reliability depends on whether procedure, weather, or social clearance is the current dominant force.

**Expected traffic:** cloth, produce, guard traffic, artisan commissions move beside petitioners, officials, short-stay labor, and rumor-bearing intermediaries.

**Enforcement presence:** Visible authority is intermittent but meaningful. What matters most is whether the party is seen before it is understood.

**Toll or customs behavior:** Documents help, but recognized sponsorship, recognizable purpose, and calm sequencing often decide speed more than paperwork alone.

**Corridor reputation:** Locals treat the corridor as functional but never neutral. It remembers shortages, enforcement moods, and who can clear delay without making it look like favoritism.

**Likely delays:** queue compression, manifest clarification, selective reinspection, weather or lane narrowing, private intervention by someone with standing

**Likely witnesses:** drovers, clerks, shrine regulars, loading hands, minor officials, short-stay traders

**Goods or bodies moving through the corridor:** cloth, produce, guard traffic, artisan commissions

**Operational use:** Use this packet whenever travel scenes need to feel socially specific rather than generically transitional.
