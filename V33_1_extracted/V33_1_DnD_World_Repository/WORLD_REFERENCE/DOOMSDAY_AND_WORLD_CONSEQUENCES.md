# Doomsday and World Consequences

## The event
Doomsday was caused when Desperation forced too much divinity through the material barrier. The result was not a single explosion but a world-structuring rupture. Elemental planes smeared against the material, arcane magic became broadly accessible to mortals, and dense doom-scar masses were left at the poles.

## Lasting consequences
- the world flips on a repeating cycle
- east and west polar doom scars produce extreme environmental and travel consequences
- sea travel is constrained by whirlpools, storms, and chained ocean logic
- limbo exists as a strange reset-world within the compressed scar tunnel
- religious and political doctrine often begins from local answers to doomsday

## What people disagree about
Different cultures do not merely remember doomsday differently; they build institutions around those differences. Some see it as warning, some as blessing, some as trial, some as proof the gods are manipulable, and some prefer to forget it through pleasure or denial.

## GM use
Use doomsday whenever you need to explain:
- why a region treats magic casually or fearfully
- why law is harsher near strategic routes or ritual zones
- why old records disagree
- why travel, weather, mutation, and prophecy are culturally central
