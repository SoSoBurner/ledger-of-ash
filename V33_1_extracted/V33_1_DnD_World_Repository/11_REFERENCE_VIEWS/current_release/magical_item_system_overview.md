# Magical Item System Overview

V33_1 adds a first-class magical item framework that treats magical objects as **persistent, object-bound reality interfaces** rather than as generic loot.

## Core rule
A magical item is a stable platform where matter, symbolic pattern, stored power, law, legitimacy, and maintenance burden are fused into a repeatable magical object.

## What a valid item entry must explain
- what the item physically is
- what kind of magical function it stores, channels, stabilizes, or suppresses
- how it activates
- who is legally or ritually authorized to use it
- what upkeep it needs
- what happens when it degrades, is misused, or changes hands
- what institutions regulate, repair, seize, bless, or destroy it

## Common item families
- weapons and armor
- regalia and oath objects
- ritual vessels and reliquaries
- tools and workshop devices
- textiles and prestige garments
- archive devices and inscription frameworks
- architectural magical components
- industrial or civic magical assemblies

## Why magical items matter in this world
They carry inheritance, status, law, sanctity, custody, and trade power. They are not neutral. Possessing the right item can change whether someone is seen as licensed, noble, criminal, sacred, or dangerous.

## Polity differences
- **Shelk:** prestige craft, ceremonial enchantment, archive-protected heirlooms.
- **Roaz:** suppression gear, evidence seals, confinement devices.
- **Mimolot:** scholarly apparatus, licensed instructional tools, research artifacts.
- **Panim:** funerary vessels, sanctified relics, afterlife transition objects.
- **Sheresh Communes:** dome regulators, powerstone interfaces, survival enchantments.
- **Soreheim Alliance:** rare strategic relics and communal industrial integrations.
