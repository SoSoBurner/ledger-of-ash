# Current Release Interconnection GM Guide

This release finishes the interconnection layer by making routes, offices, rumor, religion, hazards, and campaign continuity usable together.

## How to run it without drowning

Start with one locality packet, one route, one faction primer, and one institution. Add only the next-most-relevant consequence when the party creates stress.

## Minimal operating loop
1. Open with a starter packet or table-ready locality card.
2. Identify the visible pressure.
3. Ask which institution or faction benefits from delay, exposure, or procedural failure.
4. Decide what rumor version the public hears first.
5. Mark one consequence in the campaign packets.

## What to connect in real play
- **Locality to route:** Why does this place matter beyond itself?
- **Route to goods:** What becomes more expensive, harder, or morally loaded if the route strains?
- **Institution to fear:** What are ordinary people actually afraid will happen if this office fails?
- **NPC to office:** Why is this person more dangerous or useful than a generic quest-giver?
- **Rumor to truth:** What false-but-plausible version spreads faster than the real one?

## Good consequence discipline
Use one hard consequence and one soft consequence.
- Hard: lost access, violence, seizure, delay, sanction, route closure.
- Soft: suspicion, altered prices, rumor drift, tighter paperwork, colder hospitality.

## Using the new deliverables
- `world_interconnection_contract.json` defines how objects should interrelate.
- `polity_dependency_matrix.json` explains what each polity cannot afford to lose.
- `route_and_goods_matrix.json` turns roads and corridors into economic engines.
- `truth_layer_rumor_ladders.json` helps separate public story from actual truth.
- `campaign_consequence_propagation_ledger.json` shows how party action ripples outward.

## Keep it human
Even in a dense world, scenes should still be about people:
- the clerk who knows too much
- the porter who saw the wrong crate
- the steward whose ritual duty conflicts with need
- the student who copied what should not move
- the roadwarden who must choose reputation over mercy
