# Bestiary System Overview

V33_1 adds a first-class bestiary framework so creatures, hazards, quasi-living anomalies, sacred agents, and contamination-adapted organisms are treated as world-native pressures rather than detached monster entries.

## Core rule
A valid creature entry must explain what the thing **does to the world**. That means ecology, route pressure, settlement adaptation, law, salvage, labor, fear, and institutional response all matter.

## What counts as a valid bestiary subject
- natural fauna
- magical beasts
- contamination-warped organisms
- sacred or taboo enforcers
- route predators and migratory hazards
- site-bound emergent life
- managed war-beasts and labor species
- magical infrastructure symbiotes

## The practical design layers
1. **Biology** — body, reproduction, senses, habitat fit.
2. **Ecology** — niche, migration, prey pressure, scavenger relation.
3. **Metaphysics** — magic, contamination, planar bleed, ritual susceptibility.
4. **Behavior** — threat pattern, intelligence, territoriality, learning.
5. **Civilizational response** — fear, worship, exploitation, suppression, law.
6. **Institutional response** — who is responsible when it appears.
7. **Salvage** — what can be safely harvested, sold, forbidden, or destroyed.
8. **Narrative meaning** — what it symbolizes to people living near it.

## Why this matters at the table
This system supports more than combat. It tells you:
- why roads shift
- why walls look the way they do
- why certain trades exist
- why wardens, hunters, priests, engineers, or magistrates intervene
- why rumors spread and what locals fear at night

## Polity differences
- **Principalities:** creature handling becomes a question of law, road security, licensed response, archive evidence, and salvage rights.
- **Soreheim Alliance:** hazard engineering, communal defense, quarry safety, and production continuity dominate.
- **Sheresh Communes:** dome integrity, quarantine, research classification, and contamination control dominate.
- **Nomdara Caravan:** mobile defense, route scouting, and barter-risk adaptation dominate.
