# Storm Window Scene Driver

## Use
Deploy this when the search for Lord Myrs Shelk's daughter starts slowing down into procedure. Every scene below should force a trade between speed, secrecy, legitimacy, and exposure.

## Scene 1: The safe shop that is no longer safe
At the General Store, ordinary commerce continues too hard. One clerk is over-helpful, one customer is listening, and one item that should be in stock is missing because someone bought the whole shelf to control who lingers.

**What breaks the scene open:**
- a roadwarden arrives wet and irritated
- a Union broker notices the party recognizes the wrong name
- a servant of House Shelk spots the party first and chooses whether to claim them in public

## Scene 2: Retainer panic behind dignity
A household retainer requests a private word but keeps correcting their own phrasing, terrified of sounding disloyal in a city where words travel faster than carriages in a storm.

**What the scene reveals:**
- someone in House Shelk fears social blame more than the disappearance itself
- one lead has already been delayed to protect a reputation instead of the girl
- silence inside the manor is no longer aligned silence

## Scene 3: The expensive favor
A Union-adjacent intermediary offers transport, a messenger, or a witness location, but the price is not money. The price is narrative ownership: later they will say the party needed Union order to function.

## Scene 4: Road window
The storm gives one narrow travel window. Taking it risks recognition. Missing it pushes the next clue into a harder social layer where paperwork or noble discretion takes over.

## Escalation truths
- Delay helps the Union even if the Union caused nothing.
- Public urgency harms House Shelk even if the party means well.
- Every official witness increases the wanted-status risk.
- The daughter can remain physically recoverable while politically becoming harder to return.
