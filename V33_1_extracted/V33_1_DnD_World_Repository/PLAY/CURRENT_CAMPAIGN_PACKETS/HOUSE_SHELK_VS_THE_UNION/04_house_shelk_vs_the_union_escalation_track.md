# House Shelk vs the Union Escalation Track

## Track purpose
This track models what happens when a missing-person search in Shelkopolis stops being a controlled local matter and becomes leverage between House Shelk and the Union.

### Step 1: Quiet concern
- House Shelk still believes the matter can be contained.
- The party can move with moderate discretion.
- Public process is not yet dominant.

### Step 2: Managed scrutiny
- More eyes are on witnesses, shops, and gates.
- Neutral contacts begin asking who authorized the party.
- Small mistakes start attaching the party to one side.

### Step 3: Narrative contest
- House Shelk and Union-aligned voices are no longer merely searching; they are interpreting.
- Witnesses become harder to access cleanly.
- The party must choose between speed, discretion, and legitimacy.

### Step 4: Procedural lock
- Access requires sponsorship, paperwork, or overt faction cover.
- Useful civilians go quiet.
- Delay now actively benefits the side already better positioned to weaponize procedure.

### Step 5: Public consequence
- The search changes local rumor, household posture, and faction relations.
- Even a success now creates winners and losers beyond the daughter’s recovery.
- Promote consequences into root continuity files immediately.
