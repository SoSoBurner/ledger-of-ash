# Necromancer — Occult Hand

You begin in Aurora Crown Commune, not as a tourist of its polity but as someone who already knows
the pressure of its thresholds. Air smells of ozone, lamp oil, snow-wet cloth, boiled broth, resin,
and metal warmed too near heat sources. Quieter and more careful than temperate polities. Heat
management, repair, research, route timing, preserved food routines, and aurora or containment watch
shape daily existence. For a necromancer of the occult hand, this origin should feel native before
it feels dramatic. You feel the pressure behind words, rites, and objects, and you are harder to
fool when comfort arrives with the wrong shape. You hear pattern in the wrong phrase, weight in the
wrong blessing, and meaning in the places where doctrine and utility no longer quite agree. The
first disturbance should not arrive as a prophecy. It should feel local and survivable: a phrase
repeated with the wrong emotional logic, an object handled with too much private caution, a witness
who becomes calm too quickly, a small public correction that lands with the weight of suppression
rather than order.

The opening movement should stay anchored in Aurora Crown Commune while using Glasswake Commune, and
Sunspire Haven only as adjacent pressure. Let rumors, freight, copied records, shrine traffic, or
witness behavior confirm that the same shape of unease exists one stop away, but never let those
places overwrite the origin. The player should first learn what wrongness looks like when filtered
through Aurora Crown Commune's own habits: dome stress, contamination seepage and the cost of
keeping life ordinary under them.

Use Aurora Crown Commune Innkeeper as the first human anchor, then widen through workers, clerks,
assistants, patrol hands, shrine helpers, brokers, or record keepers. Let gossip belong to the
people who actually keep the place moving. They should speak about tensions in the language this
locality already uses: survival versus curiosity; innovation versus containment; shared shelter
versus personal secrecy. Humor should come from competence, fatigue, and dry local standards, not
parody.

If Rosalind is mentioned at all here, it should be by travelers and foreigners rather than locals: a
route captain from Shelkopolis, a scholar with fashionable ambitions, a merchant spouse talking
above their station for a moment too long. They do not accuse her of anything. They speak of her
with reverence, lust, admiration, or cultivated envy, as though House Shelk keeps somewhere in its
salons a woman who can steady embarrassment, sharpen courtship, and make private suffering look
beautiful. That rumor points toward Shelkopolis without making her a local actor.

The transition should come from an origin-rooted clue that refuses to stay contained: a copied
phrase in a record, a keepsake passed through ordinary hands, a witness naming a road mark, a shrine
worker comparing notes with a traveler. Use Glasswake Commune and Sunspire Haven as corroboration
only, never as a replacement for the origin, and let the trail tighten toward Fairhaven as the first
place where the pattern can be seen fully enough to follow.

## Transition to Stage II

Clues that refuse to stay contained leave Aurora Crown Commune through its own managed cold routes: copied records, freight consignments, shrine messengers, or witness testimonies moving between dome-watched waystations. The first leg of the journey feels long and controlled, shaped by route timing, containment protocols, and the harsh cold of the communes. Only after Glasswake Commune and Sunspire Haven echo the same wrongness does the trail tighten toward Fairhaven—the first place where the pattern can be seen fully enough to follow. Once there, Stage II unfolds through Fairhaven Chapel, Sunflower Market, The Gilded Plough Inn, Watcher’s Perch, Emberforge Outpost, and Plume’s End Outpost. However long or indirect your road, these stations remain accessible and should anchor the story as you move from local misgiving to regional corruption.
